Mestea
The Turf of Shal Omnis








Founded by the Moon Knight Niteblade, the Mestean Archipelago is a collection of islands that were dedicated to prepare for the arrival of Shal-Omnis, the sovereign deity of all Dragons. It is said that invoking the name of Shal Omnis grants instant sanctuary to any who are pursued by agents of her nemesis, Styphon. Through an act of faith, prophecy, and historical evocation, Niteblade reunited with his alter-ego DarkBlade. In doing so, the Archangel of Shal-Omnis invoked the Holy Gate to Shal-Omnis, along with her 12 roke-mage/clerics, makers of the Dragon Orbs. After her arrival, Shal-Omnis revealed the true nature of Niteblade.
The Mestean Archipelago is found in the Watersea of Worlorn. Cooperative pacts were made between cousins Theseus and Llewella allow free passage between the realms during Theseus's guardianship of Mestea. Shal-Omnis chooses to maintain this relationship.
Peridot can not exist in Mestea, for it is mysteriously Removed from the possession of the bearer until the owner summons it within the Watersea.







Mestea
The Island of the Yellow Rose








Mestea Island is surrounded by an immense, nearly impassable barrier reef known as The Great Barrier. A dome shaped membrane of pure energy surrounds the atmosphere five miles above the island. Only the leaders of the Yellow Rose, as ordained by Shal-Omnis, and dragons can find their way innately.
Mestea is one of the Dragon Isles, found in the Great Sea. Dragons and all others who desire it may find sanctuary here.














Shal-Omnis
Supreme Deity of Dragons








Shal-Omnis is the supreme mother and supreme deity of Dragons. Her clerics, trained at Roke, crafted the orbs from which she gains a portion of her power. Shal-Omnis is compassionate and seeks to united all dragons as a unified and cohesive force. Her son, Omnis the GoodKnight(AKA NiteBlade) and Theseus (also known as Gerard and Gerier in some lands) walk Worlorn, along with her 12 cleric/roke-mages. As Son and Archangel, Omnis and Theseus have united to evangelize Worlorn and bring about a new world order of unity, good, and peace.







Sanctuary
omnipresent archetype








travellers will find it hard, even dangerous, to find Mestea alone. For some, however, the risk may be well worth it, for once within the Great Barrier and the Energy Dome, one is safe from all external influences.
In the core Sanctuary of Mestea, one may petition Shal-Omnis, her son, or her Arch-Angel for full Sanctuary against all pursuers. Before you attempt this, make sure that your persecuters' claims against you are unjust, lest the Trinity rule against you.
One need not suffer boredom while taking sanctuary in Mestea. Adventure and danger can be found for those who seek it. Adventurers who are worthy may find excellent sources of training in arcane weapons and magic skills lost elsewhere for centuries.







The Sanctuary of Mestea
golden attribute focus








the high one on the Sanctuary of Mestea:
The Sanctuary of Mestea provides, supports, and controls nine-tenths of all manifestations of sanctuary in worlorn, including but not limited to Abelard Sanctuaries, Life Hutches, Leomund's Tiny Huts, Daern's Instant Fortresses, and Circles of Protection. No being within Mestea may be brought out or targeted by forces external to Mestea, without the explicit consent of the being or Theseus. No Worlorn turf can attack Mestea, and all Worlorn turfs will automatically defend Mestea against any extra-Worlorn attack.








MoonKnights and Dragons
the people of Mestea








Mestea is home to the MoonKnights, an Elite order of the Rose. MoonKnights are trained to bond and ride with Dragons, proficient in very specialized swordsmanship and dragonlance. The relationship is more of a partnership between dragon and mortal, and is not for everyone: Dragons choose their riders, not the other way around. Most of the humanoid races of Mestea consist of elves, halflings, and humans. Dragons on the isle often appear as humanoid figures. However, there is usually some physical distinguishing mark, if one looks closely. More than one elvish MoonKnight and his bonded Dragon have been mistaken for Kav Elfar at first glance.
The MoonKnights are seldom seen outside Mestea. When they are, it is almost always to right wrongs, punish injustice, or promote the express will of Shal-Omnis.

Likewise, no Dragon who seeks Mestea can be prevented from finding it. Mestea is the Sanctuary for all Dragons who seek refuge. No force in the universe can prevent this innate ability of Dragons to find Mestea's sanctuary.
