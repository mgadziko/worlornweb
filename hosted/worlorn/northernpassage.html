Inspiration originates within a large chamber beneath the ground. It is a torchlit cavern, circular and domed, about 40 yards across. There are no doorways, no physical exits. The earthen ceiling is supported by seven arched wooden beams, supported in turn by fourteen wooden pillars, tall as a man. The wood is living: polished roots of a special tree. The floor is polished black obsidian, reflecting like a mirror.
There is a design in the center of the obsidian floor of the chamber. The Pattern is constructed of laser-straight furrows an inch across, a latticework thirty yards across, apparently made from a single star sapphire. Two short furrows at each intersection are inlaid with emerald. The pathway itself is filled with black dirt. Two lines bisect the pattern: north-south and east-west, lines made from a rubberlike yellow substance.
Four wooden torches are lit, held upright at the cardinal points by clay vases floating in earthenware bowls. Ventilation keeps the chamber free of smoke, the roof free of soot. A woman or man would smell only an earthy dampness spiced with a hint of sulphur.
A woman has collapsed near the unmarked center of the room, face down. She is semi-conscious, apparently in pain. It appears she has somehow twisted her legs about each other, twisted them like two ropes tightly entwined, as if they were boneless tentacles. No chance exists that a human could walk on those legs.
There is no other wound, no Blood; Trojan Blood is harmful to an awakened Pattern.
She wears a loose wrap of white cotton, immaculately clean. Her flesh is the color of coffee-with-cream. Ears with slight points protrude slightly from shoulder length brown hair. A chance exists that she wields the flesh of her Wengen and Faerie heritage. In this place in this time she is invariably a half elf.
Reflecting both Isadora and the Doctor, the woman has a foolishly long scarf tied about her neck; it is the precise color of moonbeams in ether. Any Githzerai would believe the Scarf of Troy is beautiful, otherworldly, mystical, Holy. Each end flows from her neck to a length of near ten feet. They lie east and north of her body. The woman has many smaller scarves tied to her limbs, tied to fingers, tied to each other: silken kerchiefs of all twenty hues.
A chance exists that she wears on her head Oimota, the Crown of Worlorn. In this place at this time, no chance exists that the crown has fallen off.
Two ballet slippers have apparently fallen off the woman, for they lie near her feet. A chance exists that her left toes are still within the left shoe. In this place at this time the right shoe is always between six and eight inches from her other foot.

A chance exists that there is a Red mist to mark his passage. A chance exists that there is a Blue mist to mark his passage. A Green mist marks that this passage was unimpeded from the North. Order is important. No chance exists that his passage is unmarked.
The newcomer is Ghundian male: pale skin, midnight blue eyes, straight black hair, tied in a pony tail with a thong of undyed flesh from a cow. Despite their differences, there exists a resemblance between the man and the fallen woman. They both have tall faces with similar noses: thin and pointed without being overlarge. Their chins are well defined.
The man’s natural and supernatural attentions rapidly assess the chamber. The chance that he turns widdershins is essentially equal to the chance that he turns deosil. There is great dependency on initial conditions. His gloved hands are empty, held out flat at this hips, palm down. A black sphere a foot in diameter floats in the air behind his left shoulder.
He wears a black and red doublet with matching trousers. His head is bare, his feet covered by leather boots. Among many lesser pieces of jewelry, he wears a silver scarab that gives him a chance when none exists otherwise. A coven thammay hangs on his belt and a harp case is slung across his back. While he bears this harp, no chance exists that this man will lie.
The man’s attention circles the chamber. Afterwards he crouches to sit on his heels to examine the woman, using both his eyes and powers. The floating ball turns from black through violet to blue, nearly as vibrant as his eyes. His examination proceeds for only an instant before he reaches to her head. He sweeps Oimota from her brow. With the same motion he places the Crown of Worlorn on his own head. The man pauses only to adjust it until it is balanced properly. No chance exists that the crown will fall from his brow.
In this time, in this place, the woman does not become fully conscious. Although a chance exists that she may stir, if she does, the man speaks to her saying, "Simon says sleep.”
Once Oimota is wielded, the man resumes his search of the woman’s body. Her feet flop about like bean bags. The man grimaces at her twisted legs, and pauses a moment to regard them. The floating ball flashes black again for an instant. Afterwards, he lifts her legs gently to roll her over, and tries not to jar her legs unnecessarily.
He is perfectly thorough, displaying much practice and professional efficiency at stripping bodies. No chance exists that he will miss anything significant. He does not attempt to remove the lovely scarf, the scarf of ether and moonbeams. He moves the red shoes onto the pile of removed gear, located so his body is always between it and the woman. There exists a chance that the woman had been wielding the crown, the pretty scarf, the shoes, and two other strange attractors. in addition to removing all strange attractors, the man unties each of the scarves, as well as empties many of the clever pockets in her white wrap. Everything taken, save for the crown, he piles behind himself, well out of reach of the woman.
Once he is finished looting the body, the man says, “Simon says awaken.”
