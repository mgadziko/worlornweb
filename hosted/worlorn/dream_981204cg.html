dream for crimson grey
exhile becomes servant







Crimson Grey is on his back in a black room. the stone is cold. after a moment of dread, he comes to realize it is the chamber from the octagonal building, where he awoke alone once before. Crimson pulls himself in to the outer chamber. His guess is correct, the outer chamber lights up when his feet hit the floor. like last time, there is a chamber with a black door on one end, and his crypt on the other.

unlike last time, he is not alone. there is a hairless monkey or ugly boy-child in the room with Crimson. the child is less than three feet tall, and although fear is not the reason, the child stands as far from Crimson as the room permits. except for the metal Crimson carries, the creature's gear is identical to Crimson's. each metal item is reflected in the child's gear as stone, wood, or bone. the child÷speaks?÷without moving its lips.

"I sorry," he says. "I _sorry_."

with the words, Crimson Grey feels a wave of sorrow, feels it in his heart and soul as if it was his own pain, is own shortcomings, his own failures. the pain is like a blinding flash of hot pink light showing through eyes held tightly shut. Crimson closes his eyes and put hands over his ears, staggers against the wall and falls to one knee, but the depths of sorrow do not end.

"i so sorry," says the child once more, the only gentle thing left in Crimson's mind. the waves of regret continue like the ocean pounding a rocky shore, driving him awake, anything to escape from the dream.

Crimson lays on his bed at Salt Peter's estate, awake with eyes shut for many minutes, remembering all his shortcomings liek a roll call, each and every time he failed to meet someone's expectations, arrived late, forgot a birthday, or wronged a loved one. it is a while before Crimson can strengthen himself to rise from his bed.

when Crimson sits up, there is an ugly child—or a hairless monkey—dressed almost exactly like Crimson Grey in the room at Salt Peter's Estate. it looks so sad, standing there, staring at Crimson.
