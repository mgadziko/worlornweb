not crimson’s wish
equal and opposite







Crimson Grey is breathing. His breeze ring allows him to breathe, even when his lungs and chest do not. There is a paralysis in his chest, but no numbness; the pain is steady, if not sharp. Crimson feels the damage to his lungs as each second passes when they do not fill with air. It is hard not to wince, as he feels his right lung collapse, but Crimson can't stop to wince, he can't stop to breathe, he can't stop concentrating or he'll die. Crimson is sweating, from concentration. What happens when he tires and falls asleep?

There is no sound from the clearing. All Barrett’s men either died or were turned to stone. All the medusas plus their pet, that . . . other thing, the lizard thing, are dead, too, finally. While the poison blood may be coursing through Crimson’s veins, it’s not flowing in the lizard any more, except on its way out over the dirt and broken stone. At least the elves all got away somehow.

Crimson winces as something _changes_ in his chest, worse than a lung folding in on itself and collapsing. A sharper pain shoots from his center, from his windpipe just above his stomach, upward and outward to both armpits, and then down the arms. OK, so maybe his arms _are_ growing numb. Just a little. Crimson can feel the ring, still, and that’s what matters. He feels the ring. He feels the air it brings to nourish his body. He feels fine. He feels fine. He cries aloud as a wave of pain flows from his heart outward like ripples in a pond. He feels the ring....

"Who is there?" says a voice. "Does anyone yet live?" It is a man, speaking in one of the eastern languages, one of the languages they made parts of Thieves Cant out of. Florentine. Thieves Cant is a creole, a language made from other languages. The language Crimson Grey knows is Thieves Cant, but these words are Florentine. Crimson cries out again, groaning not to answer the man, but to answer the newest knives his heart is shooting down his arms.

The voice continues, but Crimson cannot make out the words let alone translate. All he can do is concentrate on the ring, something, anything besides the throbbing with his heartbeats, the drumming of hurtful blood his heart unknowingly shoots out to all parts of his body. All he can do is live, concentrating on the ring.

"Oh, glorious Spam," says the voice, now in Kimdissi, now from a mere foot over Crimson’s face. "This fellow is dying of hunger!" he curses in Kimdissi. Crimson opens his eyes, and sees a face without eyes, a face from which the eyes were burned, burned with a hot poker, a heat like Crimson’s blood, the blood burning thorugh his cruel and dutiful veins.

"I need some food here, _now_!" roars the man, and Crimson passes out, entirely beyond the realm of medusas, beyond poison, beyond pain, beyond food.

. . . Next to your mentor on your right is the fourth councellor, a man you do not know: he is er per'henne, has never died, and has never been psionically aware. He has, however, had the eyes burned from his face with a hot poker. The scars are interesting in profile. He wears robes, which can be deceiving . . .

Lord Barrett’s face hovers over Crimson Grey so damn close that Crimson can see the melted flesh where the tip of the hot poker scarred the back of Barrett’s right eye socket. With a sudden inhalation, more from startlement than fear, Crimson pulls his face away from the mangled flesh, and wrinkles his nose. Lord Barrett wears the scent of roses; smell was _not_ conveyed in the dreams granted to Crimson by the githzerai. Do gith have a sense of smell? wonders Crimson . . .

"You are well then?" asks Barrett in cordial tones. "I was certain they had lost you until the priests finally removed your fancy undergarment. Quite impressive, actually. Very few Relics like that are permitted to exist. Gods are finicky about such, you might imagine."

Lord Barret hands to Crimson Grey an onion bagel with cream cheeze. Suddenly famished, Crimson takes the bagel with his right hand, and then Crimson _looks_ at his fingers with horror, dropping the precious food.

Crimson holds out both hands before himself, and sees nothing, feels nothing, empowers nothing . . . wears nothing. Crimson grey has no rings, no armor, no jewelry, no gear whatsoever, he weilds nothing but the halfling body between the bedsheets.

"I'm sorry about the wind sub-ring. We didn't realize it was keeping you alive until we took it off." Barrett turns his face to his other hand, where the smokey ring is now revealed flat on his palm.

"Are you aware that once you remove it, you can never again draw power from such a device?" Barrett puts the ring on the blanket near Crimson's knees. "We didn't know what protections kept my priests from curing you, so we removed all your gear."

"I would order one of my two men with similar devices to replace yours with theirs, but that would merely switch the personality who resented me, and quite frankly, I would regret their ire more than I think I will regret yours. You I had ressurrected." The man lowers his face towards the floor for a moment.

"If you like, I can get you a class ring for this shadow, but of course that would work only until you controlled probability by another means." Barrett stops and glares at you, without eyes, much like he did to the other councillors, in Crimson’s githzerai dream. It is the set of his jaw, the angle of the brow, something about how he holds his lips that shows Crimson Barrett’s not just accidentally facing any direction.

"What are your means, Crimson Grey? If you kept a Dark Angel, one decades past Kharst I might add, kept him at bay using a child and a minstrel, why did you fall to the pet of the Samway Medusa, I wonder? What are the means of Crimson Grey?

"You clearly have the support of Spam, or you would not be so well equipped. Can you take a message to El Cano Monde for me? Don't bother to deny it, I'm Lord of the Burgundy Council. I know the title and position exist, and Occam’s Razor says that Spam Gangrene is too stupid to designate another Kimdiss to be the big bop--"

Crimson Grey hears a , such as occurrs when someone uses the feather gift of Maja Barusse or Vlad Petros, hears the and suddenly in the room there’s a third person. the newcomer is another kimdiss, but this one is wearing a smooth mirror-smooth suit of plate mail with fitted helm and black goggles. It would look so funny, the metal suit makes the kimdissi look like a ball bearing with goggles, except this hobbit is equipped with a strange weapon like a pistol crossbow, and it’s aimed at Lord Barrett’s back.

Crimson barely has a chance to cover his face with his hands. a smaller pop comes from the weapon, and Lord Barrett freezes in place, paralyzed. A pink film oozes from Barrett’s back until it covers his entire body in a period somewhat less than a second. Barret appears as if he was engulfed in bubble gum. At least his scars don't show any more.

Through the pain of his arm movement, Crimson can see the Kimdissi in plate mail aim the weapon at Crimson, but before Crimson can shit his pants, the guy lifts a finger to his lips, the universal sign of "shhhh!" Ignoring the pain, Crimson nods, the universal sign for "Whatever you want, dude!"

The shiny fellow flicks the point of the weapon left twice, and Crimson tries to grasp the wind ring as he makes to roll out of the bed. However, before Crimson grasps the ring, he’s suddenly engulfed in pain. Crimson clasps his chest and gasps, rings forgotten, collapsing half out of the bed, his belly on the mattress, but with feet on the floor.

"You've been mostly dead all day," says the Kimdissi. The fellow is looking at a tiny portable clock he has strapped to his wrist. "You get out of bed now, and we both can get back to work. Come on en en fifteen. Try it again."

It takes a minute before Crimson’s vision clears, during which he accomplishes rolling over. Crimson keeps his eyes open the whole time, but it’s as if the kimdissi had shot Crimson with a shiny yellow pill and a glowing yellow film had covered Crimson’s vision. "Screw you!" Crimson gasps. "I'm a hurtin unit," he whispers, as loud as he can.

"En en fifteen, I order you to get out of that bed now, get back to your rightful shadow, or you're fired."

"What?" Crimson opens his eyes, despite the pain. "Who are you? Why do you think I'm working for you?"

"Oh, shit!" The kimdissi raises his goggles with both hands, somehow keeps the weapon ready, and reveals his face. "Crimson, don't you know me?"

Crimson shakes his head. Another crazy time traveller. That’s all Crimson needs.

"The banquet’s been set and the kitchen’s empty!" the Kimdissi curses. "You need to go back where you belong, and no matter what, that has to be NOW." He looks a the clock on his arm again, then glances apprehensively at Barrett. The kimdissi reaches under the bed for Crimson’s pack. He throws it at Crimson’s chest. Crimson clenches the pack to himself, which starts a new round of pain, accompanied with blurred yellow vision. Crimson slides to the floor and curls into a fetal position around the pack, because it seems to hurt less that way.

When Crimson’s vision clears, the fellow has a strange stone pyramid in his left hand, about an inch on each edge with each face a different color, and he is extending his right hand to shake Crimson Grey's. Crimson doesn't even attempt to move his arm.

"Crimson Grey, I'm pleased to meet you. My name is John Roy Priddy, and I don't exist. Pretty soon, you won't exist any more either. Until then, mum’s the word, eh?"

When it’s clear Crimson doesn't intend to shake hands, John Roy Priddy looks at the stone as he does something to it with his left thumb, and Crimson Grey is catapulted into Crossing-in-the-Mist.
