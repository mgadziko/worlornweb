Adventure record - January 1999

I wake up alone in the tree. "For Derek" appears and makes me understand that I've been asleep for a while and my siblings are somewhere far away, and further, that I should use my Mark ring to exchange my location with that of Derek and Misty (who must themselves now be sleeping, and need to be in a safer place). So, "I wish that my location were exchanged with that of Derek and Misty."

I join Ryndall, Crimson, Tanri, "For Crimson", and "For Tanri". We're at the overgrown ruins of Kendol, city of the Faeries. (They've journeyed four days along the road away from the Great Oak.) Ryndall takes a small piece of the rubble, which he needs for the Keebler Mark he desires to construct. "For Tanri" suggests that any remaining faeries may not take kindly to this, so I leave a container of hard cider in its place. Indeed, a faerie warrior approaches before we can leave, but the cider placates her.

We continue along the road, with the goal of reaching the desert, where Ryndall hopes to acquire more components for his mark. In the woods, we meet a wandering Kestrel ranger, named Amar. He's hoping to learn songs from the Ironjade kethi, but they haven't invited him yet. We share bread and information with him, and he joins us on our trip toward the desert. We travel four more days.

"For Tanri" tells us that Misty has awakened. Tanri uses a feather to return to the tree where Derek and Misty are now. Crimson experiments with his wind powers to communicate with Tanri, but he's noticed by some other air power, so he "shuts down". Tanri and Misty return to the party using feathers, leaving more behind for Derek.

Amar warns us of dangers in the desert, so I pray to Dorshall for a divination. His message to me: "Do not walk in sand deeper than a sword's length. Journey into and out of the desert in a single Crossing in the Mist, leaving all supernatural gear behind. Leave Derek behind."

Adventure record - February 1999

Derek and "For Derek" wake up in the tree near the Great Oak, alone. They chat, waiting...

The rest of us wake up in our tree nearer the desert, some days away near Wunib. (4 days back to Kendol, then 4 more to the tree by the Great Oak.) Crimson uses wind power to communicate with Derek. Tanri and "For Tanri" teleport back to Derek and "For Derek", then all four return. The "air power" again notices Crimson, and Crimson now can tell that it is moving toward us, probably flying quickly.

Our journey toward the desert now leaves the forest and enters grasslands. After a day, we reach the small village of Wunib. We stay the night camped away from town, without setting up our usual Quaal tree.

After 8 more days, skirting the Enigmatic Grove (which Amar warns us to avoid), we reach the edge of the desert. In the rocky area, Ryndall sets up a tree. Heeding Dorshall's warning, Ryndall divests himself of all magic gear. Tanri and "For Tanri" do also. Misty does too, except that she finds she can't leave her sword behind, and decides to go along anyway, taking it. Derek heeds the warning, and chooses to stay back in camp with Marsellus, Crimson and "For Crimson".

But before they can leave, Crimson detects that the "air power" is fast approaching, so everyone quickly re-equips. A Faerie woman arrives in a whirlwind. She's dressed all in black. She comes up to Crimson (though "For Crimson" stands between them), and calls herself "Naga, Mistress of the North Wind". She thinks that Crimson wants to challenger her for some sort of "title" of a monastic organization. Crimson refuses to fight, but he seems unable to get her to understand that it's a mistake.

She uses some magic to put all the humans to sleep, as well as "For Tanri". Misty and I wake up "For Tanri", while "For Derek" wakes up Derek. "For Crimson" attacks Naga, as does Amar. Naga says, "No, no, it's supposed to be single combat! I'm going to report you!" She backs off, transforms into a whirlwind and flees.

Again, Ryndall, Misty, Tanri and "For Tanri" divest themselves of magic (except, again, for Misty's sword), and head south into the desert. Amar leaves us on an errand of his own, saying he'll be back in a day. We set up a tree to wait.

Ryndall, Misty, Tanri and "For Tanri" journey into the rocky desert. Ryndall is looking for something as a component for his Keebler Mark. They see the exoskeleton of some sort of giant (man-sized) insectoid creature with big pinchers and spikes. "For Tanri" says "Bone". They realize that this was the shell that was shed as the creature grew larger, and that they are crossing the trail of a larger group of these creatures! "For Tanri" says that the group travelled east.

Misty recognizes the shell's material as extremely valuable and metallic, and almost certainly more wealth than she's ever seen before. Ryndall takes a small piece, which Misty hacks away at it with her sword to take away the entire breastplate. Ryndall realizes that in the desert, they might expect such an encounter every day (and that they were extremely fortunate that this encounter was only with a molted shell!) so the group picks up its pace, moving quickly in search of open sand.

Ryndall collects some sand, and they begin moving quickly back north. Suddenly, "For Tanri" says, "Uh oh!" and a deep booming sound is heard from the south and a small speck is seen in the southern sky. They all begin to run.

It is a dragon.

As it passes far far overhead, the elves can tell that it hasn't noticed them, and is merely flying along on its own purposes. The human Tanri feels overwhelming awe and fear, and the sense that it is coming for her. Even at its great height and tremendous speed, she can see every detail of it -- black on top, sky blue on the bottom, the flexing of its great limbs, the smoke or steam coming from its nostrils. "No, not for me, not this time" she wimpers. It seems to take an eternity for the great beast to pass overhead. "For Tanri" tells her, "I sorry".

Ten seconds after it passes overhead, tremendous thunder breaks all around us. Tanri must be helped along, and "For Tanri" has vanished. They return slowly, but as quickly as they can, to the camp outside the desert.

Meanwhile, the dragon also overflew the camp. Derek's experience is the same as Tanri's, and while Crimson feels the same awe, his is mixed with wonder rather than fear. Both "For Derek" and "For Crimson" tell their companion "I sorry" and disappear.

Adventure record - February 24, 1999

Ryndall and I share a dream, where our party is being chased in a fog by unknown pursuers. In the dream, everyone else is hidden in some way except Ryndall and myself.

Waking up, we share the dream. Taking stock, we learn that everyone in our party is protected by a peridot item of some sort, except Ryndall and myself. Missy gives Ryndall her peridot item. Derek is asleep, so Crimson picks him up and brings him with us using wind power. "For Tanri", "For Crimson", and "For Derek" are still gone.

Amar rejoins us, and leads us northeast some distance to a circle of stones in the grassland. I recognize it as an alter to some non-coven nature power. On the ground is a staff and a bag containing leather origami birds. He says he tried to pick up the staff, but got a bad feeling. Misty tries to pick it up and gets the same feeling. Tanri picks it up with no ill effect, and it changes to match her height exactly. All the items are magic, and I detect that the staff and at least six of the 19 origami birds are of Good alignment.

The birds speak to me in my head, but not in a language I can understand. I repeat some of it, and it seems to be Khannish. Eventually, they speak aloud a little bit, and Tanri and Ryndall (who speak Khannish) tell us what the birds are saying: their master was taken by some supernatural power over a year ago. They've been lying here on the ground over the winter, and they don't want to spend another winter. (It is four weeks till winter begins.) I take the bag of birds and we begin travelling west, back towards the forest, with the goal of next reaching the Fountain of Salamacis, where Ryndall hopes to gain more components for his Keebler Mark.

Overnight, I pray to Dorshall for divination about the dream. He grants me a vision, which is something like the earlier dream, except that it has a much more urgent feeling. There is a man with his eyes plucked out, dressed all in courtly gear, who is searching for Misty specifically, and she is no longer hidden from him.

In the morning, I describe the man, and from the description, Tanri recognizes him as the man from her vision who leads the "Burgundy Council". Ryndall returns Misty's peridot item, and takes Derek's peridot dagger, while Tanri gives me her peridot pendant. Amar says that we are being pursued, and that his wolf companion was killed by an arrow from the pursuers. We break camp, and knowing we cannot hide in the open grasslands, head into the Enigmatic Grove, hoping to lose our pursuers.

In the Grove, we discover four Braith kethi chained to a tree. Misty frees them with her sword. The leader recognizes her and gives her his signet ring in gratitude. We continue on.

DISCONTINUITY

We stumble into the lair of a Medusa.

DISCONTINUITY

Tanri is turned to stone, though Crimson and Misty slay the Medusa. Ryndall wishes that Tanri had not been turned to stone.

END DISCONTINUITY

Tanri narrowly avoids being turned to stone, and Crimson and Misty slay the Medusa. Not knowing that the blood of the Medusa is poisonous, Misty is poisoned as she cleans her blade. I quickly cast Slow Poison on her, as Amar leaps (as if compelled) to catch Misty's sword before it hits the ground. As he catches it, he too is poisoned. As I ready to cast Slow Poison on him, as well, Crimson, too, is poisoned as he cleans his blade. Using his wind power, he is able to keep breathing, but cannot do anything else, even move or speak. I do not have enough curing miracles available to save them both. Ryndall wishes that our pursuers would have had this encounter, instead of us.

END DISCONTINUITY

We come across a clearing in the woods. In the dark woods at night, we don't notice that Crimson isn't with us. Moonlight floods the clearing, showing us three silent ghostly figures, all armed and armored. A woman is kneeling over a fallen man as another man watches. She seems to be pleading with him for help, but he refuses.

They see us. She leaves the clearing to beg us for help. As she leaves the clearing, she becomes "real", no longer ghostly. Speaking Khannish of a sort, Tanri tells us she's saying that her fallen comrade is near death. I go into the clearing to help, but I can't touch the ghostly figure to heal him. Misty tries to use her magic sword to touch and move the wounded figure out of the clearing, where we could help, but the woman misunderstands her intent. Screaming, the woman attacks Misty. I interpose myself and am struck by her glass dagger, whereupon I fall into some sort of fit, in which I jerkily dance around uncontrollably.

The other ghostly man picks up the fallen figure and vanishes as the woman falls to the ground, mourning. After a time, Tanri tells her that it's not safe in this place, but that she may come with us, if she wills. Instead, the woman sets up some sort of small magical Wizard's Tower. She dispels the fit upon me; but with the unfortunate side-effect that it also dispels the magic on all my gear.

Crimson is still missing.
