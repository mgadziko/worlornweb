Adventure record - May 15, 1998

It is four weeks after Midsummer, a week and a half before the beginning of autumn.

Our old friend Brendan shows up, with Nell, Luc, and the rest of our old hand! He brings direct orders for Molan from the Quaal himself to return to Pyethorn. Brendan also brings our acquaintence Grayleaf. Grayleaf was exiled from the lands of the Quaal for forging a weapon dedicated to another god than Nolerak. Luc explains that he is a "confused child" who may yet be returned to the true faith. Brendan brings a Mark blade, and asks that I carry it for Missy, for the day when she rids herself of Blade Cuisinart. We catch up with our old friends, then Brendan leaves with an unhappy Molan (who leaves me with his Mark ring, with 4 leaves left).

Uskar is asleep. Missy borrows the ring, and places Uskar on the disk.

With Molan gone, we have no spell to open the dungeon door from within. I give Missy the Mark ring so she can wish for that spell. She wishes "I wish that I had learned and copied into my spell book the spell for opening known as Knock." which succeeds. Then she returns the Mark ring to me, with three leaves left.

We leave Uskar in the tree on disk, and obscure the entrance with an elven cloak. Missy borrows Uskar's spectacles. We go to the dungeon entrance. When Missy opens the door, some sort of salesman named Jefferson "The Panda" Tandoor comes out and tries to interest us in his catalog wares. Missy closes the door while we bargain. Then we enter the dungeon, closing the door behind us after Missy disarms the traps. She permanently damages the lock, so that the door can be easily opened from within.

The walls, door, ceiling and floor are made of wood. We move forward, down a hallway to the left. After a distance we pass a human named Cushio, who just watches us. Around a corner, we meet a loudmouthed man who moves past us till he meets Cushio. Cushio grabs the loudmouth in a headlock and moves off. We continue on up the hallway and set up a defensible position.

A bat flies down the corridor past us, but Grayleaf kills it with an arrow. Later, two humans all dressed in black come around the corner behind us and attack us. Ryndall, Missy and Grayleaf combine to slay one. The other wounds Ryndall. I cast the miracle of Helping Hand, seeking a female hobbit bearing a nine-bladed sword. The battle ceases, and the remaining human begins negotiating with us for Grayleaf's eyes, claiming that they are worth millions of gold pieces. I cure Ryndall's wounds.

Grayleaf's negotiations again become combat. The human wounds Grayleaf. Crimson calms them both down. The human again says that he'd be paid millions for Grayleaf's eyes to a place called "The Knock Shop". The human retreats away around the corner. Grayleaf refuses to accept the blessing of Nolerak, and so I cannot cure his wounds.

Later, an old human holy man approaches from around the corner behind us. He asks to pass and we let him. As he passes the corner, he says "Oh my. A dead end. May I pass back?" Again we let him. Ryndall looks around the corner and sees a petrified Shanagate kav elfar. Missy looks with the spectacles while Grayleaf desecrates and loots the corpse, much to our shock and dismay. She sees that some of the statue's gear is magic: boots, sword, armor, wand. She looks at Grayleaf: she sees wings and glowing eyes; the handle of one of his swords is extremely bright. Grayleaf has a silver cord.

Missy opens a 4 foot long scroll tube she found in the loot, and a cloud of poison gas comes out. Greyleaf succumbs and is dying. I run over and cast the miracle of Slow Poison. He now has some hours to live. I ask whether he is willing to accept the blessing of Nolerak, and after much hemming and hawing he eventually says "Yes", so I begin praying for Neutralize Poison, Cure Critical Wounds, and Slow Poison.

Later, Crimson hears a small voice saying, "Quick! Take this! You're in grave danger!" He takes it, which turns out to be a purple crystal. Missy overhears, and notices that it's Crimson's own voice! Holding the crystal, Crimson feels much safer.

Later, Darth Vader comes around the corner and demands the plans to "The War Wagon". Crimson tears the business card he got from the salesman, and Jefferson shows up instantly. Crimson explains that the big person is looking for some plans. Combat begins as Vader finds Ryndall's lack of faith disturbing. Ryndall knocks the lightsaber out of his hand, as Missy and Greyleaf close to attack. Vader summons the lightsaber to his hand, but misses (!!!) on a 1. Ryndall hits with his redsteel sword, which only wounds Vader (!!!), but Missy misses. Grayleaf fires his bow, but the arrow bounces off Vader. Vader's next swing slays Ryndall. Missy ceases attacking and searches Ryndall for the Mark ring.

Jefferson fades out. Grayleaf switches to his holy sword and attacks, but misses. Crimson does a neat tumbling trick to get into the position he wants. Missy gets the ring and says "I wish he'd missed!" Retroactively, both Ryndall's and Vader's attacks last round missed, and Vader telekinesed the 4 foot long scroll tube to his hand. The Mark ring now has two leaves. Suddenly, Uskar has retroactively been here all along, and has the disk.

Ryndall narrowly dodges another of Vader's swings with the lightsaber, and gives him a wound in return. Ryndall wounds him again. Crimson wounds him also with the Sun Sword. Uskar shoots a kavalar wand, but the Vader suit absorbs them. Missy casts Knock, hoping to open the Vader suit, but only the nearby door opens, as well as the scroll case, again releasing poison gas. A six-armed female creature (a monster in my pocket? the mother of knives?) comes through the opened door and takes Vader away, leaving the door open. The poison gas kills Crimson, Grayleaf and Missy.

Ryndall takes the Mark ring off Missy and says "I wish they hadn't been poisoned." Retroactively, only Crimson had been poisoned (because he was still wearing the purple underwear), though Grayleaf still loses a Constitution point. Retroactively, I'm praying for Neutralize Poison, Raise Dead, and Slow Poison.

Ryndall sees a glowing sign that "Oasis" through the open door. He looks into the room, and sees no creatures, only odd glowing furniture. Then four unfriendly female faeries come around the corner, but we buy them off with my beer. Missy tries to pick pockets one of the throwing stars from a faerie, and she is paralyzed and loses a level. Ryndall yells "She's drinking it all!" The other three attack that one! After the others loot and drink all remaining alcohol in the party, they stagger off.

A misshapen human with extra growths and appendages (a Mueller) shambles around the corner. Ryndall and Uskar speak to it. Ryndall gives it money, and it goes into the Oasis. After a bit, we hear sounds of machinery. Grayleaf closes the door. After a while, the Mueller comes back out and thanks us for the copper. Ryndall tells it that the front door is open, and it shambles away.

Later, a seagull pops in. Uskar says "hi", and it replies "Wow! You talk! I never met an elf that talks before!" The seagull is quite impressed with us. Uskar asks if it wants to stay and watch, and it becomes on Uskar's shoulder. They introduce themselves, and he names himself Martin. Martin asks is Uskar wants to visit Martin's home (just for a moment), just to prove to the other seagulls that there are elves that talk! Uskar agrees.

Martin and Uskar become on the beach with uncounted other seagulls. Martin bets with the other seagulls that this elf can talk, and when Uskar does, they are very excited. Then Martin and Uskar become back with the rest of us. Uskar tells Martin that they're looking for a hobbit female, wearing blue suede shoes, with a nine-bladed shoes, who can talk. Then Martin and Uskar become in front a just such a person, on a throne. She claims not to have stolen the sword. (Martin says "Whoa! She can talk!) Then Martin and Uskar become on a pony behind just such a person. They fall to the desert sand. Again the woman denies stealing the sword. Then Martin and Uskar become on a mountaintop, in the snow, next to just such a person meditating while levitating above a blanket. Uskar explains to her that his companion Crimson had his nine-bladed sword. She hands him hers and says that she has no need for material wealth, and if Crimson has need of it he may have it. Uskar asks her name, and she replies "Haminaminumum". When she talks, her mouth doesn't move. Martin and Uskar become back with us in the llabyrinth, along with the nine-bladed sword. Martin says "She was creepy!" Then he is gone.

A dark mysterious figure slides around the corner. He names himself Nostradamus, the Sage. He asks if he may accompany us, to observe and learn, and we agree. Nostradamus warns that a powerful woman is moving toward the party, and says "I do not think it would be wise to encounter Fate this way." and we withdraw around the corner. She follows and approaches the party. All pull back against the wall to let her pass. She radiates a divine aura. She moves past us and around the corner, then fades into the kavalar statue, which then explodes into dust and she is gone. Nostradamus and Uskar chat, beginning to share knowledge.

I finish my prayers. The Helping Hand has not returned, which indicates that it did find the target person, but that she was unable or unwilling to follow. (Retroactively, we determine that I must have wished that Uskar had been along, in order that he would be with us to meet Nostradamus. This left my Mark ring with two leaves left.)

We leave the dungeon, carrying the corpse of Crimson and the paralyzed Missy. As we leave the dungeon it is night, and Missy full motion is restored. In the tree, we find a sleeping Uskar on a disk (and we still have one awake with us, with a disk!)

I cast the miracle of Neutralize Poison on the corpse of Crimson Gray, then Raise Dead, then cure him of the physical damage. Uskar delivers the nine-bladed sword to Crimson. He recognizes that it is not precisely the same sword.

Uskar consults the stars, which indicate that his mind and body will become one again if he goes into contemplation and reverie. So we all end in the tree.

Note from Wedge: There must have been a free sentient around, in order for the wish to result in two Uskars. Hopefully, there will only be one when they next begin.
