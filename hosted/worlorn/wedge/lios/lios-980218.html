Adventure record - February 18, 1998

Missy (Jil Sander?) is asleep.

We debate our courses of action. Finally, Ryndall decides we will attempt to find the bard, Canaith. Perhaps he can help us understand Missy's condition. Uskar scrys and sees the bard scrying us in a pool in a forest clearing. The bard seems to be working on a painting of one of us, but it is not complete enough to tell which.

Suddenly Uskar is possessed by something claiming to be "Sauron". It asks questions we don't even comprehend, let alone know the answers to. It asks about "Spam", and "Llewellyn" and "Necrodemus". It says it will release Uskar when he's "done". Then, just as suddenly, Uskar returns. Fernan asks whether our family is particularly susceptible to possession. Ryndall tells her that we are dreamers, and she clarifies to Uskar that dreamers should never daydream while using a scrying crystal; some legend about a wizard who shattered a crystal ball.

While I pray, Uskar experiments with the red lenses he found with Jil Sander's invisiblity cloak. He sees Missy as a gray mist, and the sword Cuisinart as another mist. He also sees Dorshal coming to grant my request for a miracle!

I invoke the name of Nolerak to remove the curse on the crystal that allowed it to possses Uskar, and it shatters. Fernan asks me to keep the shards to return to Molan, whose crystal it was.

That night, Uskar scrys with his own magic the pool's location, and sees that Canaith is still there, but is gone two days later.

Over the summer, we journey southward through Swan past Wintercastle back to Molan's cave. This takes about 10 weeks, and we arrive three weeks into autumn. We are welcomed by Molan and his students. Mikhail has gone into Swan, and Fernan is sorry we missed him.

The Glowstone are still honoring the truce. We tell Molan of our adventures and troubles of the past year. Molan suspects that the "Brein" may have been Keebler or Faerie, and the Brein very seldom leave their Mark.

Crimson leads an exhibition with his nine-bladed sword, with dancing, tightrope walking and other exploits. Everyone is impressed, but Molan forbids any future circuses (as he calls it), due to a close call.
