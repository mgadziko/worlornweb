Adventure record - January 22, 1997

4-28-4494 (50 Necrodemus) Voidcastle

Shayol (party leader) resumes dancing in the drow barracks. Wedge, Marisse, Almitra and Simon join. Fenris and Ruke high-Carsultyl are the highest ranking Childeans present; the real leaders (the Gold and Silver Generals) are absent. Wedge makes his korariel pin clearly visible (luckily, Llyr reproduced it). He says under his breath, "Thanks, Llyr". Simon says, "He says you're welcome."

Wedge, Almitra and Simon each pick up a Staff of the Magi. Shan takes a wish ring and says, "I wish at my last development I had not advanced in martial arts, but rather had advanced my crossbow skill." It apparently works, and he picks up a galvorn crossbow and five black bunny rods.

Simon says, "Everyone take a decanter of endless water." We all do. He also feeds the Childeans from his supply of Captain Crunch.

Almitra takes a wish ring and says, "I wish I had fire power equal to my water power."

Wedge takes a wish ring and says, "I wish I had rolled maximum hit points for my Blood Law Bard levels."

DISCONTINUITY
alt.Wedge is now half-drow, with maximum Blood Law Bard hit points. All the drow are now female. All the staves are gone, but the wish rings and decanters remain. The drow leaders' names are now Satheya and alt.Lorelei high-Carsultyl. Threnody does not exist; alt.Lolth owns the alt.turf of Fire. alt.Marisse has never had a crow; Simon is not here. (Almitra and Shayol and their own gear are all originals; everyone and everything else are "alt." versions. For convenience, this will be omitted from the rest of the record.)

Almitra inadvertantly says the name "Lolth", then quickly turns into a unicorn in defense. A fiery gate opens, and a woman steps through with a morganti weapon; all spell memories are lost, and we all have headaches. It is Dark Helen, the focus holder of the turf of Fire, with Anglachel.

Dark Helen tries to speak to Almitra, but they have no language in common. Wedge offers to translate. Dark Helen says, "You are Ko's messenger." Almitra says Ko's name, and a yellow robe settles upon Wedge's sholders. Dark Helen wants to know if we (especially Almitra) are there to participate in their attack on Voidcastle. Wedge replies, "We're here by accident." She responds, "Those who are not with us are against us", and begins to draw Anglachel.

Almitra discovers that her Light of the World is gone; here, her water heart is the Focus of Watersea (!) She removes us to the shore, and hears Llyr's voice, "What are you doing?" The voidskimmer is now piloted by a blind Melnibonean (from the third Elric book, "The Sailor on the Sea of Fate"). His name may be Orland Fank. There are four of us (Almitra, Shayol, Wedge and Marisse); he says we must wait for the fifth. A small female voice from within a pocket of the yellow robe says, "Tell him there are five of us." He does so, and the pilot sets sail. We set destination for Llewghund.

Apparently we are sailing on the Sea of Fate; not the Watersea, and perhaps not quite the void. Shayol spends time with the pilot, learning rudimentary Melnibonean maritime vocabulary. Almitra goes up to the crow's nest for a PRIVATE chat with Llyr. Wedge and Marisse go below deck.

(Side note: the Sea of Fate is unique across alternate universes. The DM says, "There can be only one.")

There is a small silver lizard in Wedge's pocket, who says her name is Morgan. She's not too keen to go into any Turf; apparently all eight turf owners were just trying to kill her. (In this universe, it is said that she gave the morganti death to the Muse of Dreams, which slew all Iakhlu.) She believes that Wedge was Ko's (she calls him "the Poet") agent. When he claims ignorance, she exlaims, "Did the Poet send you blind?"

Almitra and Llyr: apparently in this universe, Lolth found a way to kill the Phoenix and took over the turf of Fire. Almitra is Llyr's focus holder, and is his obedient servant. He summons her to Watersea, removing her free will. In his throne room, he takes away her Gold Card for Light of the World, since the one for this universe was apparently destroyed. He also apparently takes away all her Dust Law levels. He says, "What am I going to do with you?" and gives her back her free will, but not her ability to take game actions in his Turf. He's never heard of Chrysofar; Eressea is dead; and Tann's name is not to be mentioned. Apparently he was Llyr's prime servant before, but Almitra found a way to depose him and became Llyr's favorite. Currently, Tann is a "guest" in the dungeons of Watersea.

Meanwhile back on the ship, Shayol notices a flickering light in the mist. A fast ship overtakes us -- it is a skeleton vessel, crewed by skeletal warriors. It appears to be trying to ram us, with one of the warriors, carrying a sword, preparing to leap from their ship to ours just as they hit. (We later guess that they expected to pass through our ship, with just their leader jumping on board.) The prow of the skeletal ship is some kind of cyclops creature, whose eye is the source of the light. Just as the ship approaches, Shayol shoots and hits it with a black bunny rod fired from his crossbow; the creature dies singing. It's song makes us feel that it is dying content in a way it never expected to.

Instead of passing through, the skeletal ship rams us. Our ship is now on the Watersea. The leader is thrown, but manages to hang on to the porthole outside Wedge and Marisse's stateroom. The crew of our ship, no longer ghostly phantasms, begin trying to separate the two ships, ignoring the skeletal warriors who leap from their ship and begin slaying the crew. Shayol joins the fray. Later he deduces that as any skeleton killed a sailor, it grabbed the body and threw both it and itself overboard.

Meanwhile below decks, the skeletal leader cleaves through the side of the ship with his sword. Wedge, Marisse and Morgan go out into the hall, and Morgan "locks" the door with magic. Unfortunately, out in the hall they run into an albino minstrel (who looks just like the one from Isuame). Wedge says, "Friend or foe?" He does a triple-take at Wedge; "I morganti'd you!" he cries. This is Evingolas, who wields Killer, the lead-bladed Crosstime morganti which likes to slay nonhumans.

Marisse gets between Evingolas and Wedge. She attacks while Wedge gazes at him. He screams and interposes his blade between himself and Wedge's gaze, which allows Marisse an easier strike. She still fails to hit, which shows that he has some kick-butt magical protections.

Again we retreat, this time up on deck. As Wedge opens the hatch, it knocks into view someone who was hiding on deck. It is someone who looks exactly like Wedge, down to the yellow robe. This duplicate Wedge (Wedge2) is slain by one of the skeletons and thrown overboard. Once we're on deck, Morgan locks the hatch behind us.

The ship now has a 20 degree list to port. Looking up. Wedge sees another Wedge in the crow's nest. Both Wedges simultaneously say, "What the hell is going on?" Wedge3 (the one in the crow's nest) sees Marisse and gives Wedge a "thumbs up". Wedge trumps Almitra, thinking to provide an escape, but she prefers to leave Watersea instead :-) Morgan promises to defend us against the morganti wielder, so Almitra comes through.

Meanwhile, Shayol has been helping separate the two ships. As they disengage, the crew once again become ghostly, and the remaining skeletons scream and begin to disintegrate. We are no longer on the Watersea.

As Almitra comes through, all her carded items drop (as Llyr took away her Dust Law abilities, she can no longer wield cards). Morgan takes the Throne of Llewghund, and sits in it, taking human form (making her saving throw). She then also takes the black doll.

Evingolas appears, having snuck up behind us, but does not attack, apparently because of Morgan's protection. He says that he defeated the skeletal leader. Wedge3 comes down from the crow's nest for a SECRET discussion with the pilot, but Wedge overhears the pilot say, "We're approaching the Land of Kites." Wedge3 responds, "I'm not too keen on this!"

Everyone begins to go below, except for Wedge3 (and Wedge, who lags behind to watch). Just then we're hit by a Meteor Swarm. Evingolas parries with his sword, Almitra, Wedge and Marisse make their saves, but Morgan is toasted. Wedge goes to help Morgan, just as a swarm of "kites" flies overhead, draggin their long tails across the deck. Wedge3 grabs a tail and is carried off. Wedge dodges three of the tails, but is touched by one. Also, the skeletal leader was apparently still on board, hiding on top of the cabin (with his broken sword!) He is hit and killed by several of the kite tails.

Eventually, we dock at Llewghund. Morgan says that it is Designated Wasteland which she now rules (since she sat in its Throne). At this point, the travel time and current date are unknown. Here too, we suspend but do not end dreaming, and Shayol ends dancing. Only he may resume play, by dancing.
