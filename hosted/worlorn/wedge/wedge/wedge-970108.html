Adventure record - January 8, 1997

4-17-4494 (50 Necrodemus) Watersea

Not on an adventure, Wedge and Chrysofar wake up in Watersea. Llyr says we are creations of his Turf as best he knows how, something like replicants: if we begin and end an adventure outside a Turf which acknowledges what he has done then these bodies will disintegrate. Another can be created, but it will not have a subjective continuous linear existance. Chrysofar goes off with Llyr for a SECRET DISCUSSION of turf stuff.

Adventure begins: Chrysofar (party leader) requests emissaries from all available turfs. Crogin Jacato arrives from the Plane of the Reaper, Almitra arrives from Llewghund, Ellen Ripley arrives from Dolbadarn (invoking the Compact 11.2.1, 11.2.2, and 11.2.3), and Paris arrives from the Fountains.

Chrysofar examines her own trump card (it shows black). She dies, and Llyr makes another Chrys. Chrys trumps Threnody, fails. Crogin leaves, since the proceedings are not sufficiently interesting for him.

Chrysofar says that the Dark Horse has attacked a Turf Owner (herself), and proposes an attack upon him. Paris asks, "How?" Almitra makes a spurious (and pornographic) proposal; Llyr says the plan is secret. Apparently, though, it involves Llewellyn. Paris says he likes that part, but can't condone killing. He leaves.

Llyr "becomes" Llewella temporarily as the turf is attacked. When Llyr "returns", he tells Ripley that he is willing to teach Eressea a Turf defense against the Dark Horse which he has been practicing. Ripley leaves, and Eressea and Llyr have a PRIVATE Turf-to-Turf communication. Not too private, however, since apparently at some point they are talking about Crogin, and Yama and Shayol (who are watching) drop a "History of Crogin" scroll at their feet :-)

Wedge joins Chrysofar and Almitra on the adventure. They travel to Llewghund (which acknowledges Llyr's handiwork) then to Flambeau. Chrysofar takes the party into the wind to go the length of the hall very quickly.

Wedge fills Threnody in on the apparent occurrences. Flambeau acknowledges Llyr's handiwork, and Threnody makes a Rule (!) to confirm that this Chrysofar is the turf owner of Crosswinds. Chrysofar tells Threnody of her plan to attack the Dark Horse. When he learns that the plan involves Llewellyn and a morganti, he becomes (not surprisingly) uninterested. During the conversation Threnody tells that he lost his character d12 to the Dark Horse; Wedge corrects him that it was his d20. As the DM caught in a mistake, he awards Wedge 6ep in any Blood Law class -- Wedge chooses Magic User (so he will be able to use a Talisman of the Sphere as a defense against the Dark Horse).

Conversation turns to the lore of Mirrors of Opposition. Threnody tells that he and Tollenkov learned of one in the dungeon of Colors Layered. This dungeon was drawn by Nolzur the illusionist, and even though located in Lartroxia is not in New Troad. The creatures in this dungeon are drawn from fiction and literature, such as Harlan Ellison, Arthur Clarke, the "Kane" saga, etc. It has a d6/d6/d10 encounter chart. This is where Tollenkov got two of his elemental powers.

We begin planning an incursion into Colors Layered. Almitra mentions in passing that she had hosted the Half-Elven One (Chrysofar's beloved) in Llewghund recently, but when she as DM did not fulfill (through inattention) his wish to remain asleep until awoken by a kiss by Chrysofar, he left. Chrysofar is quite dismayed!

During conversation, Chrysofar says the name of the Dark Horse; a dark shadow passes over her arm and it disappears. Threnody sends her back to her Turf, ending her adventure.

Wedge returns to Llewghund, and ends at the Duchy (Hi honey, I'm home!). Almitra, who has been shamelessly seducing Threnody all the while, ends in Flambeau -- bouncy bouncy!

After the adventure, Duncan and Alyssa (his Bird) trump to Eressea. They are no longer children, having spent (subjective) years in Hissarlik. Duncan's news: levels 11-20 are controlled by mirror kin who will not allow "real" kin to progress. Interestingly, mirror illithids only frighten and attack mirror kin.
