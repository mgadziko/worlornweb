Adventure record - December 18, 1996

Shayol, Marlee and Wedge are in Llewghund on 4-10-4494 (50 Necrodemus). We 'elect' Shayol to be party leader (for the first time). The DM asks "Where do you begin?" He answers "Llewghund". "Wrong answer. Where do you begin?" "Troad." "Wrong answer. Three times must I ask you before I may slay you. Where do you begin?" He guesses that it has to be some place he has danced before, and answers, "Flambeau".

He vanishes from Llewghund with no say from Almitra. Threnody calls, "What was that?" Almitra answers "I think it was the Muse of Dance." "Oh. ... She's picky."

Shayol finds himself on top of a tall thin spire in a dark cave. A human comes up the spiral path. His name is Grayson; he's 40-45, wearing a black jumpsuit with a silver spider pin and a white scarf. He fills Shayol in -- this is Kolvir [at Voidcastle], the Center of the Dance. Grayson uses a Hissarlik PDA to bring Wedge in, but declines to bring Marlee: "I do not choose to meet Marlee yet."

Trumps don't work here (a Greencard deck is required), and calling Yama's name has no effect.

Shayol decides to dance, so Grayson and Wedge step off the platform onto the spiral path to give him room. Shayol's Dance of Charming is done perfectly in a way he never even knew was possible. Grayson tells him that as a dancer, he can't initiate dreaming but may only accompany dreamers. But if he ends an adventure by dancing (as he has just done), then he is invulnerable between adventures, and may begin anywhere he can imagine. Grayson uses his PDA to shut down Wedge's adventure, saying "I suggest you have Marlee begin the next adventure."

Marlee begins a new adventure, in Llewghund (she's party leader). Shayol joins, dancing, gaining complete surprise on Marlee, and with no say by Almitra. Almitra now knows that dreamers are no longer protected by Gith between adventures.

Marlee trumps to Eressea to confirm her dream number, then hooks in dream 14 Wedge. [There is another Wedge (presumably dream 20) out there.]

Marlee wants to get Maeglin back. Apparently, he led his army of 7777 Childeans and reconquered Carsultyl, then attacked Voidcastle (in cahoots with Threnody). Each had 20 rods of cancellation, so presumably the streets of the castle are littered with 'em! Signs also point to Grayson's assistance to the Hellenes; also, there's a hint that the Hellenes have "remade"(?) Helen. Anyway, it apparently Maeglin lost control of his body and fell lifeless at Voidcastle and Marlee wants him back.

Wedge trumps Maeglin. In facilitating it, Almitra summons him from her Kevin's Watch using his true name. She gets the body, but it's immediately yanked back, so the trump fails.

Marlee successfully hooks Maeglin's black doll (Crogin's Collection; Ouroboros, both sides of the Moon, and a telesterion to the Reaper); fortunately, Maeglin's still in it! Then she, Shayol and Wedge walk to Dolbadarn.

In Dolbadarn on 4-11-4494, Marlee gives the doll and a flesh sample of Maeglin to Eressea, who brings it back to life with Iss Hogai. Maeglin possesses the body. Yama has been watching this using his mirror, with Eressea's permission. He also confirms that in his turf he can consult the Compact.

Meanwhile, Wedge takes Shayol to view the Eressene hot-tub rituals. Vlad Tollenkov, Wedge's father and a close friend of Threnody, asks Wedge (Halifirien is his real name) about a rumor he's heard that someone named "Hal" has plans to cast some spell on Threnody. Wedge claims not to have any such plan; Tollenkov apparently believes him. Wedge mentions his difficulties with the Dark Horse; Tollenkov is willing to arrange for a Blood Law Talisman of the Spheres for him, but as Wedge is a non-mage, he could not use it.

Marlee, Maeglin, Shayol and Wedge return to Llewghund, and all but Shayol end at the Duchy. Shayol ends, dancing, in Yama's Dry Land (by permission). As Shayol is 3rd level, Yama now knows 3 steps of the Senathar.
