High Priestess Zillah of the Church of the Reaper explains Black Dolls to Crogin Elfslayer, circa 1987.
The black doll is part of the great wyrm, one of Tloluvin’s bones. The parts of the great wyrm are all artifacts called dragonbones. The black doll is a protection device which allows its user to become essentially immune to most sorts of attacks.

Warning!
Never place a dragonbone id into a collection that includes a Black Doll. A self-aware soul-stealing platinum-rank artifact would not be anyone’s friend. Reaper forbid that anyone put a Morganti ID into a doll.










Protections








A black doll user is bestowed with a platinum ranking against physical weapons and spells. This means spells and miracles of all professions cannot affect the user unless she wills it so. Only the powers of Coven Druids, Mages, and Archmages can affect a black doll user.
Weapons of less than platinum enchantment cannot damage the user of the doll. Furthermore, any weapon wielded against the doll-user has its wounds inflicted upon the weapon-wielder. Unlike Fireshield, there is no visible effect to warn of this, and also unlike Fireshield, weapon damage is not reflected in double.








Explanation








The wathan of the doll-user is stored in the black doll, which need not be carried with the user at all times. The doll may be left at home: distance is not a factor.
Only other dragonbones of higher rank than the black doll can isolate the user from his or her doll. If the doll-user is isolated from her body, then the flesh falls asleep when the wathan becomes isolated. The Magic Bus is one such barrier. Although the city walls of Imperial Port Enseljos are not known as dragonbones, they also isolate a doll from the body it controls; no doll-user may enter the city without bringing the doll along.
Because the wathan of the user is in the black doll, some magic items and most holy relics cannot be activated by a body unless the doll is carried on the person of the body it controls. Supernatural items that require a command word cannot be activated unless the doll-user places the doll in physical contact with the device. Artifacts with command words are often not subject to this, because artifacts, particularly dragonbones of higher order, can recognize the wathan even when it is protected within a black doll.
Priests, Paladins and Crusaders must have the black doll in their possession in order to summon their divine servant and to collect divine intervention directly from their deities. The Black Doll possession is also needed to turn undead. Priests and Crusaders with alliances to the Prince of Undead [Orcus? Nemesis? Who? Damfool oblique woman-Ed.] must have the dolls to turn undead into service.








Separating the Body








If the black doll is isolated from the body, the body falls asleep unharmed until a black doll awakens it. This is accompanied by taking the body to the doll and touching the doll to the body. Any doll-user can over take a body if this happens. The Black Doll that originally emptied the body need not be used.
A doll-user can possess more than one body, but may operate one body at a time due to the concentration required. Non-operated bodies appear asleep, and are not known to be awakenable except by the doll-user.
Once a body is put to sleep by a black doll, it can be awakened otherwise only by
  • the Muse of Dreams
  • dreamspeaking
  • dream competence
  • immersing the body in the Lake of Dreams
If the wathan of the body placed within by one of these occurrences, the Black Doll loses all powers over the body. If the body belongs to the Black Doll user, then the wathan is torn from the Black Doll and placed within the original body. The owner would then have to locate and re-activate the black doll once again.








Empty Heart Weapons








The Empty Heart weapons, (known as morganti) are capable of recognizing, detecting, and sensing a black doll user. However, a weapon of the empty heart is not automatically powerful enough to draw the wathan of the black doll user unless the weapon touches the black doll.
Warning: The weapon of Emperor Necrodemus I, Sethra Lavode Glowstone (“the greater“), and Vlad Drakulya Tepes are known to be high ranking dragonbones capable of drawing the wathan forth from a host body without touching the doll. These weapons are known as the longsword Greenbeame, the dagger Iceflame, and the oude Maarter Phaal, respectively.

Activating a Black Doll








In order to activate a black doll, the user must terminate a Crossing-in-the-Mist with no other supernatural item or relic in her possession. The next time the doll-user desires to Dream, the body may be manifested at any location which the doll-user has been before. This includes the location of the black doll, but does not limit the doll-user to appear there. After the projected body forms, it remains tangible until killed.



When a doll-user’s body is killed, the dream is over, and the Crossing-in-the-Mist is terminated. The next time the doll-user desires to Dream, the body may be manifested at any location which the doll user has been before.
Hogwash. You must acquire another body -Locnar






Multiple Bodies








If a doll user has two or more bodies, the Dream may be transferred to a new body at the instant of the other’s death.
The black doll does not allow a user to make more bodies of the original user.
If a dreaming body is touched to a black doll while it sleeps, then the body becomes controlled by the doll [and no longer by the original inspiration. -Ed.].
If an inspired body touches an activated black doll such as on a Crossing-in-the-Mists, a chance exists that the touching wathan is not controlled by the doll-user. -Ed.
