the dream of april 24th
retroactively, always true.







The High One, Troy Ounces the Half Elven One summons Waldann high-Ironjade X, the Archmage, Dylan Llyr, and Eressea the Far Traveller, naked, Called forth by their true names. None may resist until after you have arrived. None of you Four are protected by freedom of choice, none are preserved from the High One’s will by any power or knowledge. True Naming is No Matter What.

The High One speaks. “Well met. We gather here between the worlds and outside of time. Today you are reinforcements. Open your heart and mind to discover Truth.”

Dylan Llyr laughs out loud! The High One smiles and looks at each of you, presents you each with a robe of homespun cloth. Waldann wears a yellow robe. The Archmage dons red, Llyr wears blue, and Eressea is clothed in green, with a hood.

As you dress, you come to Know the Primal power here, a Foundation beneath Illusion which you have always suspected, Truth which you always hoped to discover. Now you know this Root of All by perception, you Four may evermore Protect, Preserve, Remove yourself, have the Freedom to leave: you Know this Place.

To your senses, you Four newcomers have come to a large chamber beneath the ground. There are no doorways, no physical exits. Dirt ceiling is supported by seven wooden beams, supported in turn by fourteen wooden pillars. The wood is living: polished roots of a special tree. Four wooden torches are lit, held upright at the cardinal points by clay vases floating in earthenware bowls. Ventellation keeps the chamber free of smoke, the roof free of soot.

The High One now reveals a sword in her right hand. This is a weapon of the Wasteland, the Unmaker. This is A Game of Chess. She walks deosil, clockwise, about the rim of the chamber facing outward, holding A Game of Chess at waist level, pointing the Weapon outwards, scattering salt on the ground from her left hand. You Four are within her circle.

She speaks. “I cast this Circle to protect me from all nasty and nice energies and forces on any level that may come to do me harm. I draw into this Circle only the energies and forces that are right for me and the most correct for my work.”

There is a design in the center of the obsidian floor of the chamber. The foundation of the Pattern is comprised of laser-straight furrows an inch across, a latticework made from a single star sapphire. Two short furrows at each intersection are inlaid with emerald. Wide pathways are filled with black dirt and a cross is made from a rubberlike yellow substance.

The High One speaks, “I create sacred space. So mote it be.”

The High One lowers her formality and her Weapon, and walks before Waldann. in the East. The High One says, “I may unsummon each of you and Unmake your memories of this event if this is your wish.”

Waldann says, “No way.” Four times the offer is made. After Waldann, the Archmage, then to Llyr, and finally to Eressea. Four times the offer is declined. The Archmage nods. Llyr says “‘An’ ye harm none, do what ye will.’” Eressea says, “I am yours.” You Four look at each other with a new bond, a measure of pride, and even a sense of wonder. Llyr cannot Remove his smile.

The High One moves to the East and reveals What the Thunder Said, the Weapon of the Wasteland, hanging in the air at the wall. It is a sword, it is a cyclone, it is a vortex, it is a breeze. It is What the Thunder Said. The High One lifts her left hand and it Moves to float before Waldann high-Ironjade X, in the East.

The High One speaks. “Oh, spirit of the East, bearer of freedom, keeper of the air, I call you to this circle, for your blessing.” Waldann lifts his right hand and grasps What The Thunder Said, once again a sword. The High One smiles again, and motions without words, revealing Waldann must stand at the termination of the eastern arm of the yellow cross, at the Watchtower of the East, with What the Thunder Said held at waist height, facing outwards.

The High One says, “Face the East and visualize a mist of yellow rising from the stone, spreading around the circle clockwise, slowly moving upward to form a half-sphere about you.”

The High One moves to the South and reveals The Fire Sermon, the Weapon of the Wasteland, hanging in the air at the wall. It is a sword, it is a pillar of flame, it is plasma, it is a match. It is The Fire Sermon. The High One lifts her left hand and it Moves to float before the Archmage, in the South.

The High One speaks. “Oh, spirit of the South, preserver of justice, keeper of the fire, I call you to this circle, for your blessing.” The Archmage lifts his right hand and grasps The Fire Sermon, once again a sword. The Weapon greets the Archmage with Respect. The High One smiles again, and motions without words, revealing the Archmage must stand at the termination of the southern arm of the yellow cross, at the Watchtower of the South, with The Fire Sermon held at waist height, facing outwards.

The High One says, “Face the South and visualize a mist of red rising from the stone, spreading around the circle clockwise, slowly moving upward to form a half-sphere about you."

The High One moves to the West and reveals Death By Water, the Weapon of the Wasteland, hanging in the air at the wall. It is a sword, it is a raindrop, it is a mist, it is a wave. It is Death By Water. The High One lifts her left hand and it Moves to float before Dylan Llyr, The Prince Who Was 1000, Troy Ounces made Whole, Melisse Who Came Back, in the West.

The High One speaks. “Oh, spirit of the West, remover of doubt, keeper of the Water, I call you to this circle, for your blessing.” Llyr lifts his right hand and grasps Death By Water, once again a sword. The High One smiles again, and motions without words, revealing Llyr must stand at the termination of the western arm of the yellow cross, at the Watchtower of the West, with Death By Water held at waist height, facing outwards.

The High One says, “Face the West and visualize a mist of blue rising from the stone, spreading around the circle clockwise, slowly moving upward to form a half-sphere about you.“

The High One moves to the North and embraces Eressea before revealing Burial of the Dead, the Weapon of the Wasteland, hanging in the air at the wall. It is a sword, it is an earthquake, it is a chasm, it is dirt. It is Burial of the Dead. The High One lifts her left hand and it Moves to float before Eressea the Far Traveller, True Light of the World, in the North.

The High One speaks. “Oh, beloved spirit of the North; merciful healer, protector, and forgiver; keeper of the earth, I call you to this circle, for your blessing.” Eressea lifts her right hand and grasps Burial of the Dead, once again a sword. The High One smiles again, and motions without words, revealing Eressea must stand at the termination of the northern arm of the yellow cross, at the Watchtower of the North, with Burial of the Dead held at waist height, facing outwards.

The High One says, “Face the North and visualize a mist of green rising from the stone, spreading around the circle clockwise, slowly moving upward to form a half-sphere about you.“

The High One moves to the southernmost portion of the Pattern and steps between the sapphire lines. She speaks. “With this boundary I make a barrier to hate, a protective circle, blessed by god and goddess, elemental and mortal. I call upon the God and begin the Ritual.“

The High One removes Oimota, the Crown of Worlorn from her head, and affixes it to her costume.

Troy Ounces the Half elven One draws the blade of A Game of Chess across her left wrist, and lets the Blood of Troy fall to the floor. It is a process of Hours, then Days. You Four guardians deflect all distractions. You have no fatigue, you need no rest. Time is not passing as it does other places. You are between worlds.

The furrows of sapphire and emerald contain the liquid as it dries. The Half Elven One walks backwards and allows her lifesblood to flow freely, A Game of Chess is a paintbrush in her hand, spreading the Blood evenly in the Pattern’s width. From her left hand she sprinkles the pathway with salt, or Dust, as the Blood dries. She pauses regularly to rest and heal herself, as a monk, as often as possible. She is a woman with no concern for time’s passage.

You Four guardians deflect all distractions. There is one distraction each time she crosses the dirt. There are no physical attacks, although the stress of defense has you sweat Blood until it pools at your feet.

When Troy comes to overlap her pathway, she draws the emerald inlays upward with a wave of A Game of Chess, now seeming for all the World a wand. The emeralds become mirrors which the Half Elven One paints with her blood, and then paints again with some invisible design. After blowing on the panes to dry the blood, she sprinkles then with her Dust and lowers them into the floor as they were before. Two emerald panes are painted per intersection, with both sides of each pane receiving attention. This seems to be wearying above the effort of creating a pathway.

You Four guardians deflect all distractions.

From the Center, Troy Ounces speaks. “Farewell spirit of the watchtower of the East, Keeper of the wind. I thank you for your presence here. Visualize the mists of yellow being sucked into your Weapon. Drive your sword into the yellow material of the floor and it will be yours whenever you are in the Wasteland.”

Waldann drives the sword deep into the floor. Only the hilt protrudes, and then it is revealed no more.

From the Center, Troy Ounces speaks. “Farewell spirit of the watchtower of the South, Keeper of the fire. I thank you for your presence here. Visualize the mists of red being sucked into your Weapon. Drive your sword into the yellow material of the floor and it will be yours whenever you are in the Wasteland.”

The Archmage drives the sword deep into the floor. Only the hilt protrudes, and then it is revealed no more.

From the Center, Troy Ounces speaks. “Farewell spirit of the watchtower of the West, Keeper of the water. I thank you for your presence here. Visualize the mists of blue being sucked into your Weapon. Drive your sword into the yellow material of the floor and it will be yours whenever you are in the Wasteland.”

Dylan Llyr drives the sword deep into the floor. Only the hilt protrudes, and then it is revealed no more. For a moment, perhaps, there are two hilts in the floor. Llyr, who has been Melisse Who Came Back, gasps out loud.

From the Center, Troy Ounces speaks. “Farewell spirit of the watchtower of the North, Keeper of the earth. I thank you for your presence here. Visualize the mists of green being sucked into your Weapon. Drive your sword into the yellow material of the floor and it will be yours whenever you are in the Wasteland.”

Eressea drives the sword deep into the floor. Only the hilt protrudes, and then it is revealed no more.

Troy Ounces stands wearily and salutes you Four, East, South, West, and North with A Game of Chess, a sword. She then drives the sword into the floor. Only the hilt protrudes, and then it is revealed no more.

She reveals Oimota, the Crown of Worlorn and places it on her brow. The High One speaks. “I must go to a place and rest. Return to your Wild Magic Domains, where you will awaken as if from a Dream with full memory of this Ritual.”

The High One reveals a book in her hands. “Know that You now stand between the worlds. Some of you already had this Power. Think carefully on this. Time and space are no longer the boundaries they once were. You Four guardians each will ever have the choice and ability to retroactively betray the Trust.”

She looks down to the floor and sighs. Then she is gone. For a moment, there are revealed a circle of black statues at the place where her feet had been, perhaps obsidian statues of women as large as two fists each.

Maybe nothing is revealed. Maybe it was false inspiration. Maybe it was just a Dream after all.
