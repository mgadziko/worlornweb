a dream for tanri
exhile becomes servant







Tanri Silverharp is on her back in a black room. the stone is cold. after a moment of dread, she comes to realize it is the chamber from the octagonal building, where she awoke alone once before. Tanri pulls herself into the outer chamber. her guess is correct, the outer chamber lights up when her feet hit the floor. like last time, there is a chamber with a black door on one end, and her crypt on the other.

unlike the first time Tanri was here in the room, she is not alone. there is a hairless monkey or butt-ugly girl-child in the room with Tanri. the child stands back, as far from Tanri as the room permits. the child is less than three feet tall, with ears big as tea cups. except for the metal Tanri carries, the creature's gear is identical to Tanri's. each metal item is reflected in the child's gear as stone, wood, or bone.

the child„speaks?„without moving its lips. "I sorry," she says. "My fault. I _sorry_."

with the thought, Tanri Silverharp feels a wave of sorrow, feels it in her heart and soul as if it was her own pain, her own shortcomings, her own failures. Tanri closes her eyes and put hands over her ears, staggers against the wall and falls to one knee, but the depths of sorrow do not end. the mindpain is like a blinding flash of hot pink light showing through eyes held tightly shut. "i so sorry," says the child.

the apology is the only gentle thing left in Tanri's mind. waves of regret continue like a stormy ocean pounding at a lighthouse rock, driving her awake, awake, away.

Tanri lies with eyes shut for many minutes, remembering all her shortcomings, one after another, passing in front of her mind. as if it was judgement day, all her regrets, those forgotten and those never to be forgotten, they all bow to Tanri one after another: every time she failed to meet someone's expectations, arrived late, forgot a birthday, wrote too-mocking a rhyme, or betrayed a trust. it is a long while before Tanri can strengthen herself to rise from her bed.

when Tanri sits up, there is an ugly child„or a hairless monkey„dressed almost exactly like Tanri Silverharp, standing there in the room at Salt Peter's Estate. her dark eyes are sad, so very sad.
