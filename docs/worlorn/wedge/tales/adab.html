Adab
Adab by Halifirien Wedge

Vlad gritted his teeth and continued climbing. He was sweating, despite the chill breeze blowing through his long brown hair. That last rockfall was close, he thought. I've got to be more careful.

He smiled grimly. The hell I will.

The fog was close above him, now. It stretched its cotton candy fingers toward him, reaching, but he saw it in time. Closing his eyes in concentration, the fog parted a little, revealing more of the cliff-face. Mostly hard rock with some cracks for hand and foot holds and a few brittle, withered plants clutching precariously to life. He noticed them and cursed. They reminded him of home, so many years ago. Adab, the demanding memory, came upon him and would not be denied.

Behold the young Elf-Lord, newly come to power! The land is fair, the sun is bright and warm, and his love is so beautiful. Then come the evil spirits from the dark dimension of the Demon Queen. The people are slaughtered, the lands are ravaged, the very sun blotted out from the sky. He and his young bride are taken prisoner, and he is forced to watch the Demon Queen torture and kill her, and devour her very soul. He goes mad, and does not even resist when the Queen turns to him.

Behold the foul Vampire-Lord, newly come to power! The land is dark and cold, demons roam freely, and he is alone. For an entire Age, he remains alone in his black castle. He does not remember his previous life, for he is alone.

Then, suddenly, a bright Light enters his dark domain. In anger, he sends his demons to extinguish it. Let me be alone! he cries. But the being wielding the Light destroys his demons, breaks down the gates of his castle, and advances upon him. The Light blinds him, burns him, makes him remember. Let me be alone! he cries, and throws himself at the being, desiring to tear it apart and drink its blood, as he has to so many others. Then the Light burns through him in one final, brilliant agony, and he knows no more.

He awakens, lying in a field of grass, on a mountainside in the sun. The grass is long and green, and feels good between his fingers. The sun is warm, and clouds drift slowly by.

Then he remembers! He remembers his bright land, his love. He remembers the demons, the Queen, his love's death. He remembers his dark land, he remembers being alone. He remembers what it was like to not remember. He remembers ... and the pain of it makes him scream in despair.

A hand touches his shoulder. He starts, sits up. Kneeling in the grass before him is the being wielding the Light. He marvels; the Light, the sun, they do not burn him now. The being is dressed all in white, hooded and cloaked. The being lowers the hood. It is a woman, her face shining with the Light. Gently, she touches his cheek.

As if she knew his thoughts, she says You cannot forget, Vlad Tollenkov. This pain is a part of you now; it is a part of what you are, just as my pain is a part of what I am. But remember your joy as well; it must be a part of what you are. Know this: your pain and your joy and your honor called out to me across all Shadow and I came for you.

He thinks of his dark land, but he cannot go back. There is nothing to go back to.

Adab released him. He rested for a moment to still the shaking, then continued climbing. As he got higher, the wind grew colder and stronger, and the fog became harder to control. He slowed and concentrated, lest any wisp of it touch him. The effort showed on his face, and he was drenched in sweat. Hour after hour he climbed. He didn't think of the top, the end of the climb or the darkening sky of evening, but only of the strength of each foothold, the next handhold, and driving back the fog. But at last there was no next handhold; he had come to the top.

Mentally and physically drained, bleeding from a dozen minor wounds, he forced himself to stand up straight. Slowly, he looked back down the cliff, to the swamp at its foot, over the wide river winding its way through farms and fields, then finally, on the horizon, he saw the tall castle Dolbadarn on the shore of the Sea of Chaos. A single candle was lit in the tallest tower, and he knew she was watching. She says she loves me, he thought. Oh Eressea, why did you come for me?

Standing by a candle, looking across the land with her keen sight, she saw him fight back the fog to reach the top. He has survived it once more, she thought. He would be back tomorrow without a word, as if he had never been gone. As boundless as his joy on the night of their wedding and at the birth of their son, was the depth of the pain that does not ease with time. Whenever the memory of it became too much to bear, he set off alone to pit his body and mind against the Chaos, exhausting himself into forgetfulness, and extending her lands a little farther.

Ah, my Love, she breathed softly, wishing he could hear. Know this: your pain and your joy and your honor called out to me across all Shadow and I came for you.

Looking in his eyes from across the miles, the single tear on his cheek was the mirror of her own.

(February 14, 1988)
