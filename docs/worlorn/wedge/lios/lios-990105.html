Adventure record - January 5, 1999

autumn, Maja's mansion in Isuame

Tanri, Crimson, Ryndall, Derek and Marsellus awake. Misty and Uskar remain asleep. Tanri has a discussion with Crimson, and I with Ryndall, while Derek converses with his githzerai. Tanri then brings Derek up to date and explains their situation with the githzerai. Missy wakes up, and speaks in tongues with the gith to try to get their aid for our plan to adventure in the wizard's tower (called Home By The Sea). The gith, to protect Tanri, Derek, and Crimson, refuse to help and make clear that they will prevent them from "the probability of finding the place".

While considering this turn of events, Tanri and Crimson relate to us an identical dream they both had, in which they saw Maja's father, Saltpeter, as a member of a powerful council they called the "Burgundy Council". They say that they council has been watching us disapprovingly, especially Crimson, Derek, and Tanri, less so the lios siblings. They say that Sharra was also there, but disagreed with the others, and by the end of the dream she had stormed out, quitting the council. Tanri is very sure that this was a True dream.

Maja comes in, saying she overheard her father's name. She says her father is dead, and that the Burgundy Council was only a conspiracy theory her father studied many years ago and had dismissed as false. Nonetheless, Maja provides us with magic devices for protection from scrying, all except the sleeping Ooskar, who has one already, and myself.

Thwarted from going to the Home By The Sea, we decide to help Ryndall on his mission to collect items he'll need to construct a Keebler Mark. We travel east from town two weeks along the road and another half week along a path in Shando forest. The journey is astonishingly uneventful (as if Ryndall already had a mark! -- perhaps the gith were involved), except for an encounter with black bunnies. Crimson and Tanri tell theirs not to slay the bunnies, but Derek stands back and allows his to kill all nine bunnies.

Approaching our goal, the Great Oak, we set up a Quaal tree for the non-lios to stay in, while we three siblings approach the coven in the neighborhood of the tree and their village of Charzen. The coven are very friendly, and open to Ryndall's request for help, so long as we stay for the six days of celebration leading up to the Low Equinox. I trade brewing tips with the Shando, who are renowned for their hard cider, while Missy and Ryndall engage in (shall we say) more rustic entertainments with the handsome and beautiful residents of Charzen.

Meanwhile back in the Quaal tree, Tanri, Derek and Crimson are visited by a man who puts his head through the tree with the greeting, "Howdy, howdy, howdy!" Derek and Crimson's gith are wary, but Tanri's seems to trust him. He says he is "Richelieu's son's son", Archdruid of the trees, and is a very informal but quite charming dude! He plays the banjo, and he teaches her some of the rudiments. They trade some songs, and he is very impressed with how quickly she learns -- it appears that if "a probability exists" that she can learn it, she does! He teaches her a special song that will win bets in Bardhold. Meanwhile, Crimson teaches his gith how to use his Ring of Air. Derek's gith cleans and tans the rabbit skins and constructs a rabbit cloak for Derek.

On the evening of the Low Equinox, we three siblings share in the coven ceremony around the Great Oak. Ryndall sings a quiet song, and the tree allows him to take the piece of bark he'll need for his Keebler Mark. Missy sings and is allowed an acorn. I chant quietly, "Help for the children of Releya when we need it" and get enough oak leaves for the material components of three miracles. The next morning, take leave of the coven and rejoin Tanri, Derek and Crimson. The Archdruid is gone before we return.
