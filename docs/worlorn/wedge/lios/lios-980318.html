Adventure record - March 18, 1998

Uskar checks out a bunch of scrolls from the cave; some are histories, some are magical, one is a book. Molan teaches us how to "puff up" (in his words), to manifest our elvish aura, and says that we should do this while we are in the human city. He also tells us that he is known as "Nightingale" in Ashertiri, and we should not call him by his name.

We set out for Ashertiri. After two weeks' journey, we approach the city from the North. When guards see us, we "puff up" and they do not challenge us. "Nightingale" leads us through the low side of town to the island in the center of the river. At the bridge, guards bow and salute to Missy. Nightingale is surprised. By the time we are on the island, we have acquired an "honor guard". They call her "Highness" and say they will tell the Duke she is here. As we are leaving the other side of the island, a guard asks Missy, "Will my Queen bring her companions to court today or tomorrow?" She responds "Tomorrow" and they let us pass.

Tamara and Crimson discuss dropping out of the party for a day or so, so their faces don't become known in "official" circles. Ryndall says he should meet us in three days, north of town. The two non-elves take their leave of us, and we continue to the other side of town.

In a tavern, Tamara asks Crimson where he met the Queen of Garfieldland, and he allows as he didn't know she was! She fills him in on the local thieves organizations, then gives him a tour of town: low side, high side, and the "Maze" (the island is the really low side of town). She introduces him to her organization, the Superthieves. They put him through "entrance exams" for a day to judge his skills. They say that he is good at "making housecalls", and teach him the secret guild handshake.

Meanwhile, Nightingale leads us to an elvish inn in the Jeweler's Quarter. He arranges rooms for us, and meals to be brought to our rooms. The rooms were made for the comfort of lios elfar.

Note: this town seems to have a mixture of phoenicians, teyned and non-teyned kav elfar, "mulatto" elves, half elves, with some humans and a few hobbits and dwarves, but no lios.

We go to Missy's room, while Nightingale stays down in the public areas for a while. When he returns, we discuss how to act in the Duke's court. Nightingale tells what he knows of Jil Sander, Queen of Garfieldland, and how she should act.

During the night, a pair of phoenician guards bring invitations to court. (We won't need to use them, since the Queen can show up without one, so we stash them for possible future use.) I cast Know Customs on one of them (the male samuarai), so I am able to add to Nightingale's knowledge. In the morning, we request a large escort. Ryndall leaves his bundle, Nightingale leaves the Oaken sword, and Uskar leaves the disk and his backpack of scrolls. I assist Missy in dressing appropriately as a Queen. I also dress in city clothes, while Nightingale, Ryndall and Uskar dress in clean lios clothes.

In the afternoon, the honor guard arrives and escorts us to the Duke's court. Missy rides in a palanquin. The court is a huge translucently-domed coliseum, that holds perhaps 10,000 persons, more people than we have ever imagined could be in one place. The throne is in the center of the floor. The Duke is dispensing justice. This continues for roughly an hour, followed by a half-hour "intermission" for refreshments.
