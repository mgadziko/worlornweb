Adventure record - March 2, 1999

Late autumn in the Enigmatic Grove, in a portable Wizard's Tower in a clearing. Ryndall, Marsellus, Misty, Crimson, Tanri, Derek, along with Amar the ranger.

When we ended last, Crimson was missing. He has reappeared somehow, though naked except for his backpack. Derek for some reason is dressed in a white gown. The tower is round and high-ceilinged (in fact, it appears to be open to daylight at the top), with a fireplace and two doors, with lots of statuary and decorations with dragon or lizard motifs. There is a circular stairway around the chamber; Amar follows it up till it ends in what turns out only to be a blue-painted ceiling. There is no apparent lighting source except for the fireplace; nevertheless the room is brightly lit with few shadows. The mysterious woman who made the tower is not present.

Crimson appears deathly ill, but is also mighty pissed at Ryndall. He chews Ryndall out as only an angry hobbit can. He says that we encountered a medusa's lair, defeated her but some (including Crimson) were poisoned; at which Ryndall wished that it had happened to our pursuers instead. Problem was -- Crimson was (as always) wearing his purple underwear, so the wish did not affect him! He says that he died, but that the man with no eyes (whom he names "Lord Barret") brought him back to life, after removing all his gear. How Crimson returned to us is not clear, nor how he kept his backpack, but we're glad to see him. Amar recognizes the name "Lord Barret" as some sort of Imperial noble. Barret was blinded by elves whom he prevented from slaughtering a large number of half-orc children.

He is still gravely wounded, however, so over the course of the next day I pray for the miracles to heal him, while he rests, recovers, and eats. Ryndall returns Derek's peridot dagger, then he and Amar go off to do some spell research. Derek begins training Misty in magery, and Tanri composes music.

The next day, Tanri, Misty and Crimson knock on the door that does not appear to lead outdoors. The room is the woman's bedroom. She is on the bed, still mourning. Speaking with her, they learn some of her story:

She is called "Faella", and she is from a distant "Shadow" (Crimson seems to know what this means) where there is a great war against illithids, but their side is apparently doing very poorly. How well can you do, she says, if whenever there is any probability of tripping and falling onto your sword, that is exactly what happens? She and the two men, Timor and Gareth, fought the illithids, but Timor changed sides and she had to kill him. When we found them, Gareth was making sure that Timor's skull was buried. Why is not clear to us, but it seems so obvious to her as to be not worth mentioning. The "Moon Goddess" seems to have something to do with the portal (or whatever it was) the bridged her Shadow and ours, but as the Moon Goddess also had changed sides, Faella wasn't allowed back. Gareth knew this, which was why he refused to help Timor or leave the clearing. Faella is now in despair, sure she can never return to her rightful Shadow to continue the fight.

She is initially hopeful when she learns that Crimson is "Kin of Troy" and has a "Trump Deck". But when she determines that Crimson is not an "initiate of the Pattern" and that his is a "Blood Law Deck", she decides she will have to learn how to live in this Shadow. Sending Crimson out of the room, she dresses and rearms herself. As we have always been a party of elves and a hobbit, and human thieves in elves boots, Misty notes that Faella (graceful as she is, and she is) will be the hardest of our party to travel quietly in secret, Faella bristles and says, "I fight for Shal-Omnis! I don't hide in the dark!", and makes clear to all that she is a Paladin to the Shal-Omnis, God of Dragons!

Returning to the main chamber, we discuss where to go, and how to get there. Given his recent experience, Crimson is not eager to travel again through the Enigmatic Grove, but we don't seem to have much choice, if we want to keep the party together. We also voice concern that our pursuers could be right outside the door waiting to ambush us, for all we know. Faella directs us to follow her up the stairway to the ceiling. She opens a trap door out onto the roof. On the roof, we can see that there are indeed people down below around the tower. They appear to be elves, neither lios nor kav, like the city elves of Ashertiri. Two seem to have been turned to stone; perhaps these are our pursuers?
