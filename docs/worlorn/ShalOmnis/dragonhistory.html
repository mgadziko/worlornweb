Prologue

Greetings to all whom read these words.  I am Methuselah, Ancient of the most Ancient of Dragonkind.  Herewith, let my words be a record of what was before and what is to come. 

Chapter 1

Have you ever met a good red dragon? They exist! Before the mortal coil, before time, Dragons ruled supreme within the universe, paying little notice of the fledgling mortal races that were spawning on the sandy shores beside your oceans. Dragons were united and truly good, serving the goddess and mother of all Dragonkind, Shal Omnis · the Dragon of Light.

There are eight major families within the Church of the Dragon.  There are also eight minor houses corresponding to the lesser and hybrid dragon types. Each house is ruled by a Royal Dragon of pure blood under her holiness Shal Omnis. Those eight major houses are listed below:

Platinum Star (Platinum, Platinum-Colored Dragons)
Night Star (Black Diamond, Black-Colored Dragons)
Golden Star (Gold, Golden-Colored Dragons)
Ruby Star (Ruby, Red-Colored Dragons)
Silver Star (Silver, Silver-Colored Dragons)
Diamond Star (Diamond, White-Colored Dragons)
Copper Star (Copper, Copper-Colored Dragons)
Emerald Star (Emerald, Green-Colored Dragons)

The current list of eight Royal Dragons the preside over the Eight Houses of Shal Omnis are listed below.

Kymarcnar, Dragon Supreme of the Platinum Star
Styphon, Dragon Supreme of the Night Star
Zarethustra, Dragon Supreme of the Golden Star
Flametongue, Dragon Supreme of the Ruby
Lythander, Dragon Supreme of the Silver Star
Frostwind, Dragon Supreme of the Diamond Star
Nova , Dragon Supreme of the Copper Star
Emeraldus, Dragon Supreme of the Emerald Star

Chapter 2

Royal Dragons are easily distinguished from normal dragons as their bodies are formed by the actual element of the house (Gold, Ruby) rather than merely the color associated with each house (Golden, Red).

Once approximately every 4500 years, one new royal dragon is born to a house.  For a royal dragon to be born, the blessing of Shal Omnis is required.  The exact details of the ancient and holy ceremony is not for publication, however what is know is that each leader of the house must participate in a contest of honor, skill, and noble virtues to win the coveted blessing of Shal Omnis.

Chapter 3

How did dragons first begin their relationship with the mortal races? Why did dragons take notice in these infant races? There was one dragon who saw the vision.  One dragon who saw their potential.  Kymarcnar .

Kymarcnar was a dragon whose thoughts were always looking ahead, always looking to the future.  His curiosity for the unknown potential was unequaled in dragonkind. Kymarcnar looked down one day at the fledgling humanoid races, struggling to survive amongst the elements and evil. He saw something in this primitive race, something that sparked his curiosity.  With a little help, mortals could become a force throughout the world, he thought to himself.  Kymarcnar wanted to shape that potential ... guide it towards good to help the mortal races. Kymarcnar was a wise dragon who realized that the sight of a dragon would only spawn fear and misunderstanding.  So he researched and studied, experimenting with magics and elements. Soon he has mastered humanform.  And so it was Kymarcnar that first taught dragonkind how to assume humanoid form.

Kymarcnar brought his vision to Shal Omnis and the eight houses and convince them to aid the mortal races, teaching them how to survive and live in harmony with the universe.  And so it was, for 4000 years, dragonkind taught the mortal races their secrets, teach magic and philosophy and sharing knowledge throughout all the lands. And with the teaching of religions, some began to worship Shal Omnis along with the Dragonkind.  Soon churches were being formed in every land and non-dragon priest began to spread the word of the dragon. And so it was Kymarcnar who brought the knowledge and vision of the dragons to the world.

Chapter 4

Styphon, Royal of the Night Star, was considered by many to be the most noble and beautiful of dragons. His glistening black eyes were said to mesmerize those who stared into them for even a moment. Proud and majestic, Styphon was truly a dragon amongst dragons.

The time had come for the blessing of Shal Omnis, the birth of another Royal Dragon in the royal blood line. There were two leading contenders for the blessing, Kymarcnar and Styphon, and opinion was equally divided as to who would win out. In Kymarcnar's favor was his work promoting the mortal races.  For Styphon, he had upheld and promoted dragonkind's increasing enhancement of knowledge and power and he had strong support within the royal houses. The Ruby and White Stars supported Styphon while the Golden and Silver stars supported Kymarcnar.  The Emerald and Copper stars remained, auspiciously, neutral.

The competition stiff and the ceremony lasted longer than any other in recent history. At the end, Shal Omnis bestowed her blessing upon Kymarcnar for his work with the mortal races. Styphon argued that his work for dragonkind far exceeded the work of Kymarcnar for these "pathetic mortal races," but in the end, the decision stood.  A new royal dragon was born to the Platinum Star.

Chapter 5

Evil, Death, and UnDeath.  Ancient enemies of dragons and mortals alike. Those forces would not stand by and watch good and life triumph. It was only a matter of time before they would strike. Kymarcnar knew this ... it was what he didn't know that would rise up to wound him.

A secret society had formed within some of the mortal races. Those who mistrusted the dragon and their help ... who sought to continue the old ways of necromancy and worship of evil, death, and unlife. The orcs and drow races united with this new evil -- this new source of ultimate power.

They called themselves "The Hand of KaaraThule" or more commonly, "The Hand."  Through ancient rites and rituals, The Hand brought forth the ancient evils, led by the DeathKnights and Demonlords. An undead and mostly dead army was secretly formed in the Northern most lands.

The attack came swiftly and without warning, catching dragonkind and the non-aligned mortal races completely by suprise. The Army of the Hand slaughtered thousands in the first hours of the battle, adding the dead to their numbers. For a moment, the future of the mortal races seemed in dire peril.

Kymarcnar was the first dragon to rally the mortal races to rally and make a stand against the undead forces.  Summoning dragonkind from all of the houses, he began training mortals and dragons alike to form a unique bond, where the dragon master would allow a mortal to ride on his back. A new weapon called the DragonLance was created for this purpose. These dragon riders were called Rose Warriors of the Dragon, or simply "The Rose".  The yellow, white, green, black, and red Rose armies were formed and together, they began to push back the swarming hord of undead.

Nearly 3/4 of all the mortal races and countless dragons perished in The War of the Roses.  In the end, Good and Dragons won out ... driving the DeathKnights and Demonlords far back into the Northern lands and underworld, far out of the reach of the mortal races. For mortals, it was a time of great rejoicing.

Chapter 6

Sometimes, Victory is in the Eye of the Beholder ...

The war over, the mortals rejoiced in their own magnificience.  There was much celebration.  The human, gnome, halfling, dwarven, and elven generals were celebrated and honored, annoiting them as Kings and Princes.  And so, the First Council of Kings was formed.  Kymarcnar was given an honorary seat on the council. Kymarcnar basted in the glory ... increadibly proud of how his vision had come to be foretold.  Dragon and Mortal ... working together for the advancement of all that was good and true. This was his greatest moment.

Chapter 7

The End of an Era ...

The first meeting of the Council of Kings occurred in a keep outside of MystHaven.  The kings sat together at a circular stone table, laughing, drinking, and planning for the future of their lands. Without warning, an event occurred that would change history forever. 

A ball of energy and acid tore through the wall of the keep.  All within were consumed in an acidic fire that burned for three days.  Kymarcnar, injured on his right side, escaped the meyhem. It would be NiteBlade that would discover this chamber many centuries later, but that is another story.

The mortal races immediately distrusted the other, believing that one had betrayed the other. The fragile union that had been formed so storngly by war, was torn apart by war... The War of the Races.  Things would never again be the same.

Who? Why? The questions haunted Kymarcnar. "I did it!" The voice was clear and familiar.  "I killed your precious Council of Kings, I told the DeathKnights your precious secrets.  Dragons were never meant to work beside these pathetic mortals ... if anything, those mortals should bow down to us ... worship at our feet.  You Fool, You Hopeless Fool. And the mother ... Shal Omnis.  She cares more for them thus us.  We will no longer worship her ... We will never again sit in this house as she plays favorites to you and your whims. I am a god ... I am the GOD of Dragons!!!  Now, watch me in my glory!!!"

It was the voice of Styphon. The white and ruby stars fell with him that day, as they tore the house in two.  Styphon, Frostwind, and Flametongue, along with their houses, became utterly EVIL ... no longer protected by the love and grace of Shal Omni. She cast them down from the stars to the earth, where Styphon set himself up as a god.

Kymarcnar was devestated. He blamed himself and even with Shal Omnis's forgiveness and understanding, he pulled back from the mortal races. He would watch, from afar, but never again walk amongst them. Kymarcnar was never the same again. His heart and spirit wounded by Styphon's actions.

-----------
Shal Omnis longs the return of Styphon and his fallen Stars ... She keeps their rooms open in the Church of the Dragon.  All they must do is return to the fold and renounce their evil ways. Someday, Shal Omnis willing, the will return and the house will be whole again.
